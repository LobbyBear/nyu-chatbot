Q: How do I get health care in the US? A: NYU's [Student Health Center](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness.html) (SHC) is a campus resource and service center for all matriculated students that offers [virtual care services](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/services.html) and has [locations](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/contact-us.html) in Manhattan and Brooklyn. We offer universal, hassle-free medical and counseling services at either no cost or very reduced cost to all matriculated NYU students, regardless of insurance coverage. Our goal is to add great value to the NYU experience for students and great comfort in knowing a safety net for basic healthcare exists at NYU. If you have a medical concern, call the SHC at (212) 443-1000 and ask to speak with a nurse. Due to COVID-19, it is not advisable to visit a medical facility unless absolutely necessary. [Counseling and Wellness Services](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/services/counseling-services.html) (CWS) can help with any mental health concerns you might experience, including stress, anxiety, alcohol or drugs, eating, depression, family issues, suicidal thoughts, and more. We offer individual counseling, group counseling, wellness workshops, referrals, and long-term psychiatric medication services. Except for psychiatric services, they are provided at no cost. The [Wellness Exchange](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/wellness-exchange.html) is your greatest mental health resource at NYU. Call the 24-hour hotline at (212) 443-9999, chat via the Wellness Exchange app anytime (iPhone or Android), make an [appointment](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/services/counseling-services/counseling/short-term-counseling.html), or "stop by" during our [virtual drop-in hours](https://www.nyu.edu/students/health-and-wellness/services/counseling-services/counseling/urgent-counseling.html) to speak with a certified counselor about any day-to-day challenges or health concerns.
